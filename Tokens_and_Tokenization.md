 Think of tokenization as the bridge between human language and the mathematical world of LLMs. Since computers can't "feel" the meaning of a word, they need text broken down into numerical IDs.Here is a quick breakdown of how this works under the hood:1. How Text Becomes TokensMost modern LLMs (like GPT-4 or Gemini) use a method called Byte Pair Encoding (BPE). It doesn't just split by whitespace; it looks for the most frequent patterns of characters.Common words: Usually get their own token (e.g., " the" or " apple").Complex/Rare words: Get chopped up (e.g., "tokenization" → ["token", "ization"]).Whitespace & Punctuation: These are also tokens! A space before a word often changes its token ID.2. The Efficiency Trade-offThe "chunking" method is a sweet spot between two extremes:MethodProConCharacter-levelVery small vocabulary; can spell anything.Huge sequence lengths; hard to learn long-range patterns.Word-levelEasy to understand.Massive vocabulary; cannot handle typos or new words (Out-of-Vocabulary).Subword (Tokens)Efficient & flexible.Can be slightly counter-intuitive for math or spelling.3. The "Context Window" RealityThe context window is essentially the model's RAM.If a model has a 128k token context window, and you provide a prompt that is 130k tokens long, it will "forget" the beginning of the conversation to make room for the new input. This is why managing token count is the primary constraint for developers building complex AI agents.
