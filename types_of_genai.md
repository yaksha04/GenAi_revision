Text Generation (LLMs)These are the "logic engines." They process and output sequences of tokens. Beyond just chatting, they are heavily used for Code Generation (like GitHub Copilot), which has fundamentally changed how software engineering and DevOps pipelines are built.2. Image Generation (Diffusion Models)Most of these use Diffusion. The AI starts with a canvas of random static (noise) and gradually "denoises" it into a clear image based on your prompt.Use Case: Rapid prototyping, marketing assets, and creative brainstorming.3. Audio & Music GenerationThis is split into two worlds:Speech: Models that turn text into incredibly human-like voices (TTS).Music: Models like Suno that understand music theory, lyrics, and arrangement to create full 2-minute songs from a single sentence.4. Video GenerationThis is the current "final boss" of GenAI. It requires the AI to understand not just what things look like, but physics—how gravity works, how light reflects off moving water, and how objects maintain their shape as they move.Status: Most tools currently produce short, high-quality clips (5–10 seconds), with longer cinematic generation being the next major milestone.The Future: MultimodalityThe trend is moving away from "single-task" AIs. For example, Gemini 1.5 Pro is natively multimodal. This means instead of using one AI for text and another for video, you can feed a 1-hour video file directly into the model and ask it questions about specific scenes, or ask it to generate a script and the accompanying images in one go.
